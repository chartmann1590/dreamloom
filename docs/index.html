How it works

Three quiet steps from waking up to understanding what your night was trying to tell you.

i.

Capture

Tap the moon. Speak or type while the dream is still vivid. Nothing uploads.

ii.

Decode

Gemma runs on your phone — Hexagon NPU, OpenCL GPU, or CPU — and produces your interpretation in seconds.

iii.

Reflect

Open the Atlas, follow your symbols across weeks, and let Insight surface the patterns you couldn't see alone.

Privacy is the product.

Dreams are some of the most personal text a person ever writes. Dreamloom treats them like that.

  • Dream content stays local. Stored only in an encrypted Room database (SQLCipher) and app-internal files.
  • No account. No sign-up, no profile, no cloud sync of dream text.
  • Network use is narrow. First-time model download, ads, and (opt-out) Firebase analytics & Crashlytics.
  • Telemetry never includes journal text. Only counts and coarse flags — and you can turn it off.
  • Open about what's where. Source code is available; the brand-promise tests fail the build if these invariants break.

Frequently asked

If your question isn't here, open an issue on GitHub — we read every one.

Is my dream text uploaded to the cloud?

No. All interpretation runs locally on your device using Gemma via LiteRT-LM. Dream text is stored only in an encrypted local database and is never sent to any server.

Do I need an account?

No. There is no account system. Your journal lives on your phone, full stop.

Does Dreamloom work offline?

Yes. After a one-time model download, journaling and interpretation work fully offline. The only network calls are the model download, ads, and (opt-out) analytics.

What devices are supported?

Android 9.0 (API 28) and newer. The app auto-selects the best available backend: NPU (Hexagon) → GPU (OpenCL) → CPU.

Are there ads?

Yes — Dreamloom is free, and supports itself with non-intrusive ads (AdMob with mediation). Ads never see your dream text. Ad placements are frequency-capped so they stay out of the way.

How big is the AI model download?

Roughly 2.6 GB on disk for the int4 Gemma model used by Dreamloom. After that, no further downloads are required to use the journal.
